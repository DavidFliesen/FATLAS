# FATLAS-001 Intelligence Briefing

## Research Question

Why does the body preferentially store fat around the abdomen in some people but not others?

## Executive Summary

FATLAS-001 identified six major biological pathways that appear to converge on visceral abdominal fat accumulation:

1. Insulin Resistance & Hyperinsulinemia
2. Cortisol Signaling
3. Chronic Low-Grade Inflammation
4. Adipocyte Dysfunction & Fibrosis
5. Aging, Hormonal Shifts & Cellular Senescence
6. Gut Microbiome Crosstalk

## Central Hypothesis

Preferential visceral fat accumulation may be driven by a self-perpetuating cycle involving altered adipocyte sensitivity to insulin and glucocorticoids, chronic low-grade inflammation, gut microbiome disruption, age-related hormonal changes, and genetic predisposition.

## Top Research Priorities

- Compare visceral and subcutaneous fat behavior
- Investigate 11β-HSD1 cortisol amplification
- Improve insulin sensitivity
- Study adipose inflammation
- Investigate microbiome-host signaling
- Develop adipose aging biomarkers
